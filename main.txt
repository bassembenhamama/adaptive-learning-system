Abstract
In the era of digital education, the shift from static content to personalized
experiences is essential for learner success. This thesis presents the design and
implementation of an Adaptive Learning System integrated with an AI
Tutoring Service. The system dynamically adjusts educational content based
on each learner’s progress, strengths, and weaknesses. Developed within the
Software Engineering specialization, the project uses a Java-based
architecture to handle complex logic and data processing. The theoretical
proposal defines a formal system architecture, while the implementation
focuses on the interaction between the AI tutoring service and the learning
environment. Preliminary results suggest that the adaptive approach improves
learner engagement compared to traditional linear models.
Keywords: Adaptive Learning, AI Tutoring Service, Software Engineering,
Java, Personalized Education.

General Introduction

Project Context
In the era of digital education, the shift from static content to personalized experiences is
essential for learner success. Traditional educational platforms often provide a one-sizefits-all approach, which fails to accommodate the unique pacing and comprehension levels
of individual students.

Problem Statement
Current Learning Management Systems (LMS) in local universities primarily serve as static,
administrative-driven document repositories. They lack the real-time adaptation and
dynamic routing necessary to address a learner’s specific weaknesses, often resulting in
disengagement and suboptimal academic performance. Furthermore, existing international
AI solutions often operate as closed ecosystems with a high risk of AI hallucinations.

Proposed Solutions
In this work, we propose the design and implementation of a modular Adaptive Learning
System (ALS) integrated with an AI Tutoring Service. The system aims to dynamically
adjust educational content based on each learner’s progress while maintaining strict
pedagogical safety.

Contributions
The main contributions of this thesis are:

1

▶ The design of a Software Product Line (SPL) architecture to ensure the system remains

modular, separating core LMS features from variable AI extensions.
▶ The integration of a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pipeline to provide

proactive, zero-hallucination Socratic remediation.
▶ A dynamic routing engine that adjusts learning paths based on real-time assessment

data.

Document Plan
This thesis is organized as follows:
▶ Part I (State of the Art): Chapter 1 presents the theoretical background, synthesizes

existing platforms, identifies current gaps, and outlines our proposed system vision.
Chapter 2 details our methodology (UP and Agile Scrum).
▶ Part II (Contributions): Chapter 3 presents the preliminary analysis and detailed

structural design of our system. Chapter 4 showcases the technical realization and
final deployment of the Co-Learn platform.

Synthesis of Existing Platforms

To justify the architectural choices of the proposed Adaptive Learning System (ALS), it is
necessary to evaluate how current market leaders attempt to integrate artificial intelligence
and personalized learning [1; 2; 4; 5].
Before comparing specific platforms, Figure 1.1 establishes a taxonomy of the current
educational technology ecosystem. The landscape has evolved from static, administrativedriven Learning Management Systems (LMS) into algorithmic and AI-powered cognitive
tutors. As illustrated, the proposed ALS introduces a hybrid approach: it combines the
structural reliability of a traditional LMS with the dynamic capabilities of an AI tutor,
utilizing a Software Product Line (SPL) architecture to maintain modularity.

To understand the immediate operational context of this project, it is necessary to first
evaluate the domestic landscape. Table 1.1 provides a comparative analysis of the Algerian
E-Learning and EdTech market. Currently, the local ecosystem relies heavily on purely
administrative databases or static, “one-size-fits-all” document repositories. The proposed
system aims to fill this specific gap by bringing safe, dynamic adaptation to the local
university level without relying on foreign, black-box infrastructure.
Conversely, while the local market lacks adaptive features, international platforms have
heavily integrated algorithmic routing and Generative AI. Table 1.2 evaluates these global
leaders. While platforms like Khanmigo and Duolingo offer advanced personalization,
they often operate as closed ecosystems and present varying risks of AI hallucinations.
This comparison justifies our approach of using a local, modular system with strictly
bounded AI.

1.2

Identified Gaps and Opportunities for Improvement

By comparing these domestic and international platforms, two primary architectural and
pedagogical gaps become evident, which the proposed ALS is designed to resolve:
▶ The Rigidity of Current Ecosystems: Traditional LMS platforms are monolithic and

difficult to adapt, while commercial AI tutors operate as closed, proprietary systems.
The proposed SPL approach resolves this by rendering the architecture modular;
institutions can toggle AI features on or off without destabilizing the core platform.
▶ The Risk of AI Hallucinations: Unrestricted conversational AI poses a risk of

generating false information, which is detrimental in an academic setting. The
proposed Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) architecture mitigates this by
strictly limiting the AI’s knowledge base to the verified course materials uploaded by
the instructor.
Table 1.3 synthesizes these observations to highlight the unaddressed intersections in
the current market, directly justifying the architectural choices of the proposed ALS.
